Participation model

One Ecosystem.
Three Roles.

TRIBE26 connects communities through a layered model — every person, business, and location plays a distinct, valued role in building a stronger local network together.

Member
Consume & Connect

Anyone who joins TRIBE26 is a Member. Discover local businesses, access contributor services, follow World Cup activations near you, and earn value within the ecosystem simply by engaging. You don't need to create or host anything — you benefit from what others build around you.

Contributor
Create & Earn

Contributors are validated skill-providers: chefs, tutors, trainers, designers, translators, therapists, or any service professional. Offer your services to Members through the platform, earn recognition and revenue, and grow your reputation within the TRIBE26 economy.

Node / Anchor
Sustain & Power

Nodes are physical or digital locations that power the network infrastructure. A bakery, café, pharmacy, or exchange hosting a Totemian Brandboard becomes an Anchor Node — a trusted, visible community hub with reach across the entire ecosystem.

Circular Economy

Value Flows.
Nobody Leaks.

Most platforms extract value from communities and send it elsewhere. TRIBE26 is built on a different principle: value generated inside the network stays inside the network.

Members engage → Contributors earn and grow → Nodes gain visibility and foot traffic → the ecosystem attracts more Members → and the loop continues. Every interaction, every screen impression, every transaction feeds back into the same community that generated it.

This is not charity — it is structural design. When a local bakery hosts a Brandboard and earns network recognition, that recognition drives Members to their door, which funds their capacity, which strengthens the whole ecosystem. The network is the engine.

Local-first Value retention Community dividend No extractive middlemen Web3-ready infrastructure Transparent contribution scoring
Society 6 Framework

The Sixth
Sector.

Modern society is organised around five established sectors. TRIBE26 is built on the conviction that a sixth has emerged — the networked community — and it deserves real infrastructure, not just a social media feed.

When neighbours transact with neighbours, when a local contributor earns through the same network that screens his community's events, when a pharmacy hosts a digital board and becomes a neighbourhood anchor — that is not a marketplace. That is a living society.

What this means for you

Whether you are a first-generation immigrant building a service business, a student offering tutoring, or a long-standing merchant wanting to reach your neighbourhood — TRIBE26 gives the sixth sector its first real infrastructure in Greater Vancouver.

Community Roots

Built With
Persian Roots.
Open to All.

TRIBE26 grew from deep roots in the Iranian-Canadian community across Greater Vancouver — one of the most entrepreneurial, educated, and tightly connected communities on the North Shore and beyond.

That origin gives the platform its values: collective trust, family-level accountability, and commerce that respects the human relationship first. But the door is open to every community, language, and background. The network is richer when it is wider.
